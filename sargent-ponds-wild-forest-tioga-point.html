Tioga Point on Raquette Lake

In Challenging Hikes

Tioga Point is a New York State Campground located on the northeast shore of Raquette Lake; a fine spot for a rest and a picnic. The trail to Tioga Point covers 6.5 miles, each way, but it is mostly easy walking.**

Directions - From Long Lake drive towards Blue Mountain Lake, at the Deerland curve, turn right on the North Point Road (Signs point to Buttermilk Falls). Follow the North Point Road for approximately 7.5 miles to the trailhead for Lower Sargent Pond on the left. Note, this is the second Sargent Ponds trailhead. The trail to Upper Sargent is about 1.5 miles back down the road. A general description of the Sargent Pond trails is found here.

Difficulty - Moderate to Difficult. The terrain is mostly flat (by Adirondack standards) and there are only a couple of short boggy stretches to navigate, but the 12 mile round trip distance makes this an all day hike for most people.

Family-Friendly - The length requires a level of stamina and motivation that are out-of-reach for many families.

Winter - The Sargent Ponds trails are well-suited for either snowshoes or skiing and Lower Sargent Pond is an excellent skiing destination. Other than the distance, there are no obstacles preventing a trip all the way to Tioga Point.

One Thing to Know - New York State acquired much of the land that makes up the Sargent Ponds Wild forest around 1900. Some portions where logged before that, but old growth stands of White Pine and Red Spruce are seen along the trail to Tioga Point.

Sargent Ponds Wild forest is one of those out-the-way places that makes the Adirondacks special. Hidden among the ponds and low mountains are stands of old-growth forest and the trail to Tioga Point provides access to one impressive area.

Map showing the old growth forest area relative to Lower Sargent Pond and the Tioga Point Trail.

To visit the old growth stands first hike the 2.5 miles to Lower Sargent Pond. Continue past the pond on the Tioga Point trail. The Tioga Point trail covers an additional four miles to the New York State Campground at Tioga Point on Raquette Lake. However, this is not a drive-in campground. Campers reach it by boat on Raquette Lake.

Along the trail to Tioga Point

Tioga Point, located on the eastern side of Raquette Lake, is a nice place for a picnic, but, by itself, it might not be worth a 12 mile round trip hike. More interesting (for forest aficionados) are the impressive forest stands you pass through along the way. In particular, about half way between Lower Sargent Pond and Raquette Lake the trail crosses a small stream. This crossing marks the edge of a stand of large White Pine and Red Spruce. That combination usually means that the area was never logged, though the exact circumstances can be hard to pin down. You need to poke around off trail to see them all, but a few dozen White Pine with diameters greater than 40 inches are present in this area.

The forests all along the route are mature and attractive making this trail a must for those with an interest in Adirondack Forests.
