Rock Pond Mountain - Cedarlands

Posted by hikelladmin in Challenging Hikes Near Long Lake, Uncategorized

Wed 24 November 2021

Drive Time from Long Lake - 15 Minutes

Hiking Time Round Trip - 8 Hours

Difficulty - This is a ten mile round trip with serious bushwhacking and difficult route finding. If you manage to stay on the route, this is a strenuous hike. If you get off the route it becomes very difficult, very quickly. An old Boy Scout trail goes to the top of the mountain but this trail is difficult to follow.

Family-Friendly - This route is not family friendly. Some local hiking guides include a description of this mountain but fail to warn you that this is a very difficult hike. Not for families.

Winter - As a winter route this is for experts only.

One thing to Know - The Cedarlands Easement is closed to the public from June 22 through August 24th.

Rock Pond Mountain is a difficult hike, and not recommended for, really, anyone. The route is not marked so you need to find your own way and there is no way to the top that does not require some intense bushwhacking. The Boy Scouts had a trail to the top but it disappears on the steep cliffs just below the summit. You can make your way around the steepest parts but great care is needed. All of this and it's five miles one way, making for a ten mile day.

The views, however, are spectacular. So if you have the skills and determination to reach the top this can be a rewarding climb.

There is a trail along the ridge line that goes from the overlook to actual summit about a quarter of a mile further along. When heading down it's tempting to follow this trail past the point where you intersected it. But that leads down the wrong side of the mountain to the camps in the Moose Pond area. So you will want to leave the path and retrace your steps.

Driving Directions: From the Long Lake town beach, drive toward Tupper Lake on Route 30. After a half mile, turn right on Kickerville Road. Follow Kickerville Road to where the pavement ends. Signs indicate that the continuing gravel road is private, but that is not correct. After .8 miles on the gravel road a DEC sign points to a parking area on the left. From the parking area, return to the road and walk along the road for an additional four tenths of a mile to an intersection of three gated roads. Take the hard left. Signs indicate that this is the way to Mud Pond.

Parking: There is a large off-road parking area with trail register
