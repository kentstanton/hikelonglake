Upper Hudson Ski-Loop

Posted by hikelladmin in Easy Hikes, Newcomb, Hudson River

Mon 01 February 2021

An easy to moderate four mile loop. Marked for skiing, the route is for intermediate skiers. For hikers this is a easy walk with some climbing. A stretch along the Hudson River offers numerous places for a rest or a picnic.

Hiking Time Round Trip - Skiers should Plan on 3 to 4 hours depending on conditions and skill level. Strong hikers will need 2 to 3 hours to complete the loop and families should plan for 4 hours.

Difficulty - Skiers: This is an easy to moderate loop of just under five miles. A few of the ups and downs can be challenging, especially if conditions are less than ideal. Most people ski the route counter-clockwise. For hikers this is an easy to moderate route.

Family-Friendly - For hiking, yes. There are several good spots along the Hudson River for a break or a picnic.

Winter - This trail is marked as a ski route.

Directions From Long Lake, drive east on Route 28n towards Newcomb. As you approach the village, watch for the Essex Chain Lakes sign on the right (south side of the road). Follow the Goodnow Flow Road for five and half miles. The parking area is marked by a small sign on the right. If you cross the Goodnow river bridge (below the dam) you have gone too far. The parking area is just before the bridge.

One Thing to Know - New York State acquired the 19,000 acre Essex Chain Lakes complex from the Nature Conservancy in 2013. The management unit takes its' name from the 8 ponds and small lakes that make up the Essex Chain. You can read the DEC's Unit Management Plan for the area here.

Along the Upper Hudson Ski Loop. Great conditions, late January 2021.

The Upper Hudson Ski Loop descends about 200 feet from Goodnow Flow to the Hudson River. Designated as a ski trail (but also a good warm weather hike), most skiers follow the arrows that point the way to a counter-clockwise circuit of the loop. Going that way the trail descends towards the Hudson with the Goodnow river close by on your right. A couple of the drops are of moderate difficulty, but the grades are generally easy.

The middle stretch of the route, along the Hudson River, is the most difficult section for skiers. There are a few short but steep ups and downs that can be tricky. After about .8 miles along the river, the route reaches an abandoned logging road where the climb back to the parking area begins in earnest. This section is wide, and the grade is moderate, but you'll climb nearly 200 feet above the level of the start. The runs down to where the loop rejoins the access trail is one of the best parts of the ski.

Hikers can follow the route in either direction and will probably want to find a nice spot along the river for a break or a picnic.
